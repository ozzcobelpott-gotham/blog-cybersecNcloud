Hi!

You’re entering the “About Me” section, so let me tell you a little about myself… I currently work as a software engineer and cybersecurity researcher focused on Red Team operations, cloud automation, and backend development, with experience in AI development and automation.

My current approach isn’t just about breaking systems, but understanding them from their architectural foundations, whether it’s an exploit I found on GitHub or the WinAPI for MalDev. I have experience translating complex technical vulnerabilities into quantifiable business risks, so I can design automated solutions to mitigate them.

What I Build#

I specialize in custom tool design, reverse engineering of processes, and backend development for servers. Some of my recent areas of focus include:

  • Offensive and CTI Tool Development: Building ultra-fast search engines to parse and extract credentials from stealer logs (operating on threat actor ULPs).

  • Architecture and Backend: Implementing software design patterns (Factory, Builder, Prototype) to create modular systems. I recently developed engines for extracting and classifying financial data using OCR, debugging and optimizing the parsing of complex JSON in production environments.

  • Infrastructure and Cloud: Managing and automating cloud environments, operating directly from the terminal with Vim to maximize deployment speed and efficiency. Deploying and maintaining VPC, EC2, and S3 environments on AWS, and maintaining and setting up C2 (Command and Control) servers to simulate internet-connected threat actors.

The Vision#

My long-term goal is the convergence of these disciplines: to create AI-powered, automated penetration testing and defense architectures designed to protect both on-premises infrastructure and Active Directory environments.
